Acupuncture

Dating back around 4000 years from China, Acupuncture is based on energies through the human body and the balance in these energies are believed to be essential to one’s health. The two energies guiding the acupuncture is the balance in the Yin and Yang.

Besides, the equilibrium in the yin and yang, TCM is also based on the concept of the flow of energy throughout the body; this concept is also known as the Chi or Qi. The disruption of the energy may hence cause, pain and internal disease.

By stimulating the acupuncture points the procedures releases any obstruction in the energies, by creating a balance between the yon and yang and restoring the Qi.

Acupuncture is performed by inserting needles in the patient’s skin and carefully selecting the acupuncture points depending on the patient’s condition and goal. Additionally, the acupuncturist may use electrical stimulation, heat, a massage also known as the Tui-Na, use of herbal ointments, pressure or cupping.

Medical studies have reviled that acupuncture aids in easing muscle and skeletal pain and complications. Some of the common musculoskeletal conditions highly successfully treated after acupuncture include; knee pain, complications from extreme sports activity, neck pain and lower back pain. The needles, which can also be combined with electrical stimulation, can help modulate the autonomic nervous system inducing a release of different neurotransmitters within the body.

Besides pain, acupuncture has also been proven to help relieve, stress, anxiety, depression, severe headaches to mention a few.

A thorough health background check, before proceeding with the treatment, the practitioner needs to understand your health background to customize the best treatment for you.

The practitioner will examine your pulse and the shape, color and coating of your tongue. After the assessment, you will lay on the acupuncture table to proceed with the treatment. The acupuncturist will then proceed to insert the needles in the selected acupuncture points. You might feel an ache as the needles are inserted; however, the pains subside in a few minutes, and the process is thereafter painless. The needles are left in for around 30 minutes.

The treatment takes approximately 30 minutes while the whole process takes about an hour. Your first appointment may take longer, due to a more in-depth health history intake.

This varies with every patient, depending on your goal, health history and severity of the condition. Patients with severe conditions need several sessions, from one or two sessions in a week for several months. Many have regular treatments because they find it so beneficial and relaxing.

Before embarking on the sessions, your acupuncturist will access your health and determine what works best for you.

Quite low, the risks associated with acupuncture have over the years decreased. The needles used in acupuncture are regulated and approved by the food and drug administration (FDA)and require them to be:

  • One-time use
  • Sterilized
  • Biologically compatible

Acupuncturists are required pass a state or national exam, as well as acquire a license,

There are few severe side effects associated with acupuncture. If done correctly, acupuncture creates a balance in one’s health and wellness. However, some of the harmless and common side effects to expect include:

  • Minor bruises
  • Mild bleeding and sore muscles

Peri-Operative Acupuncture

Although you can still book and have a successful procedure, it is best to book an appointment before the surgery after discussing any surgery details.

Acupuncture is safe within 24 hours after your surgery.

Acupuncture is beneficial in relieving stress, and anxiety as well as boosting your immune. Having acupuncture performed before surgery is thus useful, although dependent on the goals that want to be met.

There is no definitive number on how many times one can have acupuncture, and it also depends on the outcome that is desired. Results vary for each patient, therefore the practitioner will assess what protocol is best and create a customized treatment plan for each patient.

Pain during acupuncture is a common concern, especially if one is experiencing pain or has swollen tissue. However, acupuncture doesn’t have to be performed on any inflamed or swollen areas, and the procedure can be performed as the swelling or injuries heals over time.

Yes, besides relieving stress and anxiety, another benefit is reducing scar tissue. However, for the best results, it is better to get the acupuncture performed sooner after the surgery.
